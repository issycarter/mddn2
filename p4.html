What are radio waves?

Radio waves are a series of repeating peaks and valleys that cannot be seen by the naked eye. It makes up the basic building blocks of radio communications that is used in deep space communication. The entire radio wave pattern is called a cycle, and a wavelength measures the distance on how long it takes to complete one cycle.

The amount of cycles that repeats in per second is known as a frequency that is measured in hertz (Hz). The radio spectrum range can be between 3 kilohertz and 300 gigahertz depending on the amount of frequencies per second. NASA and JPL often operate using high radio frequencies from one transmitter antenna to the other, whether its in space, or on earth. This is also known as optical communications, or Lasercom. This is needed when we are passing planets like Jupiter!
